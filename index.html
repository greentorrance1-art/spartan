Cardio Engine

Running Progression

Roadwork is non-negotiable. Every session. Mayweather ran 5 miles before anyone else woke up. You run before work. Every day. The base is built on consistent volume, not heroics.

Days 1–7 · Base Establishment
3 Miles Easy · No Sprint Work
Conversational pace. If you can't speak in full sentences, slow down. Goal: consistency over 7 days straight. Track pace. Note your baseline mile time.
Days 8–14 · Volume Addition
3.5–4 Miles · Add 4 × 100m Strides
Still easy base pace for main run. After completion: 4 × 100m at 80% effort, walk back recovery. This teaches your legs fight-pace movement.
Days 15–20 · Interval Introduction
2-Mile Warmup + 6 × 200m Intervals
Intervals at 90% effort. 90-second rest between. Cool down 1 mile. This is where your conditioning starts separating from the average person.
Days 21–30 · Fight Pace
4 Miles Base + 8 × 200m · Off Days: 5 Miles
Add a hill day once per week (off day). Push the intervals harder. Time your mile. Target: under 8:30 by Day 30.
Days 31–40 · Volume Peak
4–5 Miles Base + Sprint Pyramids
Sprint pyramid: 100m / 200m / 300m / 200m / 100m with 60-sec rest. Off days: 6-mile long run with surges every mile. You're building a gas tank now.
Days 41–55 · Fight Simulation Cardio
3-Minute Effort Intervals · Mimic Round Structure
Run 3 min hard (85–90%) / 1 min walk. Repeat 8–10x. This mirrors a boxing round. Your cardiovascular system learns to recover in the corner.
Days 56–60 · Taper + Sharpness
3 Miles Easy + 4 Strides
Pull back volume. Maintain sharpness. You're not building here — you're staying fresh for sparring and fight readiness assessment.

Jump Rope Protocol: Add 10 min jump rope daily (before bag work). Progress from basic bounce → alternating feet → double-unders by Day 30. Jump rope is your warm-up AND your conditioning tool. Non-negotiable.

Psychological System

Mental Conditioning

  1. THE NIGHT-BEFORE LOCK
    Every night: lay out running clothes, fill water bottle, set alarm. No decisions at 5am. Decisions are made at 9pm. The morning version of you is weaker — remove his choices. Clothes out. Shoes by the door. Alarm set. Done.
  2. ZERO NEGOTIATION RULE
    When the alarm goes off, your feet hit the floor in under 5 seconds. No bargaining. No "5 more minutes." That voice is the enemy. The moment you negotiate with it, it wins. Alarm sounds — feet down. That's the whole system.
  3. WORK SHIFT VISUALIZATION
    During downtime at work: 2-minute visualization sessions. See yourself throwing your jab. See yourself slipping a punch. See a specific combination landing clean. Your nervous system doesn't distinguish between vivid imagination and physical practice. Use this.
  4. FATIGUE REFRAME PROTOCOL
    After 8pm shifts you will not want to train. This feeling is the training. Every time you train when you don't want to, you're building the mental architecture of a fighter who performs under duress. Tired in round 8 of a fight? You've trained for this exact moment.
  5. DAILY REVIEW (5 MINUTES)
    Before sleep: What did you do today? What was the specific skill focus? Did you execute it? What do you work on tomorrow? No journaling required — just answer those 3 questions. Your progress is tracked in your body, not in a notebook.
  6. COLD EXPOSURE AS DISCIPLINE DRILL
    End every shower with 60 seconds cold water. Non-negotiable. This is not about the physical benefits — it's a daily reps of choosing discomfort. The same mental muscle you use to stay in the cold water is the one you use to train on exhausted days.
  7. PHONE LOCKOUT WINDOW
    No phone first 30 minutes after waking. No phone 30 minutes before bed. These are protected blocks. Social media and the news are stimulation that costs you energy and focus you need for the gym. Protect your attention like it's a physical resource — because it is.
  8. THE FIGHTER'S IDENTITY SHIFT
    Stop thinking "I'm a guy who works out." Start thinking "I'm a fighter who works a job." That identity shift changes every small decision. A fighter doesn't skip roadwork. A fighter doesn't eat garbage. A fighter doesn't negotiate with the alarm clock. Become the identity, and the behaviors follow automatically.
Adaptation System

Recovery Protocols

💤

Sleep Optimization

Target: 7.5 hours minimum on work days. 8–9 hours on off days. Sleep is when your body adapts to training. There is no supplement that replaces it.

  • Room: 65–68°F (cool)
  • Total darkness or sleep mask
  • No screens 30 min before bed
  • Consistent bedtime ±30 min
  • Magnesium glycinate 400mg nightly
```
🧊

Contrast Therapy

Post off-day sessions. 3 minutes hot shower → 60 seconds cold. Repeat x3. Drives blood circulation and accelerates muscle recovery.

  • Hot (as hot as tolerable)
  • Cold (as cold as tolerable)
  • 3 cycles minimum
  • Always end on cold
  • Available: ice bucket for feet/calves
🔁

Active Recovery

Friday (off day 2) is your active recovery day. Light movement only. No intensity.

  • 30 min walk or light jog
  • 20 min full-body stretching
  • Foam roll: calves, quads, lats, thoracic
  • No heavy bag, no sprints
  • Shadowbox 10 min max (loose, fluid)
🤸

Nightly Stretch Routine

15 minutes every night. Non-negotiable. Flexibility = injury prevention = consistent training.

  • Hip flexors: 60 sec each
  • Hamstrings: 60 sec each
  • Shoulder cross-body: 45 sec
  • Thoracic rotation: 10 reps each side
  • Neck rolls: slow and controlled
📉

Overtraining Signals

Know the line. Pushing through soreness is training. Pushing through these signals is injury.

  • Resting HR elevated +10 bpm
  • Sleep quality deteriorating
  • Motivation drops sharply (not laziness)
  • Joint pain (not muscle soreness)
  • → Take an extra rest day. No hesitation.
💊

Supplementation

Minimal. Evidence-based only. Food first.

  • Creatine monohydrate: 5g daily
  • Magnesium glycinate: 400mg nightly
  • Vitamin D3: 2000–4000 IU daily
  • Protein powder: fill gaps to hit 160g
  • Electrolytes: on double-session days
```
